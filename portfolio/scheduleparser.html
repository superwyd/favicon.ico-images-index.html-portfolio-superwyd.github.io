ScheduleParser


iOS App Design

Jan 2017

ScheduleParser can help you parse your course schedule into your calendar in just one click.

Inspiration

This is the project I did during a Hackathon on campus. At the beginning of each term, not only myself, but also most of my friends always cannot remember the schedule of every course and sometimes cause unexpected inconvenience. If there is an application can let us add course schedule to calender easily so that we will never miss any class or meeting, that would be awesome! So we decided to make this app.

Prototype

Here attached the original detailed wireframes I sketched:



I designed two types of homepage. The first one is kind of classical and formal in regular iOS app homepage form. The second one is more modern and consise. Both my teammate in Hackathon and 10 other different kinds of person I asked preferred the second one. Actually I also think ScheduleParser is not an app with very complicated features, so the homepage doesn’t have to include much information.

And once user types the link of the website contains course information, the app will jump to a tab with embed webpage, user can just go to the actual course schedule page and finish parsing course schedule into calendar with one click on the “Parse” button.

The final page will display whether schedule-parsing is successful or not.

During the Hackathon competition, we only had two days to finish a project. Because of the limitation of time, after getting agreement on the wireframes, I used Sketch App to create the prototype as shown below.





Testing

I asked 14 person I met during the Hackathon to help me with the mockup testing. While observing them using this app, I found out that everyone could figure out how to use immediately. Two of them suggested me to change the “parsed” icon. They thought the green icon here is a little bit disharmonious. After considering their suggestions carefully, I decided to use only simple graph instead of the combination of graph and words.
