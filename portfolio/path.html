Path


Web Development/Design

Feb 2017-present

A place for everyone to ask and answer all kinds of learning path. Users are also very welcome to share their own learning path here.

Inspiration

Nowadays we have many kinds of websites providing platforms for users to ask questions and share experiences such as Quora and Zhihu. But many friends around me always complain that if they want to search a specific learning process on these sites, the results are sometimes confusing, they still have no idea where to start learning. So Shihao Xia and I want to make a website to solve this issue by letting users share learning paths step by step. In Path, users can search for the learning path they want and check out the results in a much clearer way.

Competitive Analysis

Path Quora Zhihu
Overview A question-and-answer site focus on step by step mode. A question-and-answer site where questions are asked, answered, edited and organized by users. A question-and-answer site where questions are asked, answered, edited and organized by only Chinese users.
Competitive Adventage Users can find and follow learning path step by step clearly, so that they can learn in a more efficient way. Quora has developed its own algorithm to rank answers, which works similarly to Google PageRank. At the beginning, user registration was mostly invatation-only, with referral needed from a couple hundred of registered users
Target Market User who Ask: junior in some area.
User who Answer: senior in some area who can provide clear learning path.		 Real name users with titles and can speak English. High-profile Chinese entrepreneurs and public intellectuals.
Weakness Answer should only be step by step, which is also an answer limitation. Only questions which have been considered carefully before post have more chance to be answered. Highest-voting answer may sometimes misleading.

HomePage

While sketching out the wireframe of the home page, I used different color representing different areas and provided three designs as follows:





I did a preference test on those 60 person again and let them choose their favorite and most dislike home page design as well as give me choosing reason and feedback. The result turned out to be 29 testers preferred the first design, 4 testers preferred the second design, and 27 testers preferred the last one. The number of testers who preferred the first one and last one are too close.

Also in the feedback comments they provided, I realized that those testers who didn’t like the first design mostly because they thought the user-info section floating on the right was redundant; those testers who didn’t like the second design mostly because there were too much information on the homepage and the main part was not straightforward enough; those testers who didn’t like the third design mostly because, although the interface looked clean, the position of searching bar was a little bit discordant. And another two common feedbacks were the postion of “DownloadApp”, “About US” and “Contact Us” are distractive and the position of top three buttons “Ask” “Answer” “Share Path” were not comfortable.

Based on all the information I gained, I modified my home page with the fourth design and then created the mockup base on it and built the prototype with HTML and Bootstrap CSS framework as follows:





Once I had the prototype, I did a testing on my 60 testers again and gained lots of useful feedback from them:

- Topic tags attract too more attention than the main contents.
- Vote feature is inconspicuous.
- ReadMore button seems discordant and a little bit mess.
- Add button in navigation bar is confusing if don’t drop it down.

Based on their feedback and my reconsideration, I designed a new version of homepage. Everything looks nice to me except the vote bar. I came up with two kinds of vote bar and did an A/B testing on them as follows:





The result gaining from my testers showed that, 80% of tester who are elder than 25 preferred the right version (B), while the preference between left and right was 50% and 50% for testers younger than 25. As the consideration that our website was large age range oriented, I picked the right version.

When I thought everything all set, I just came up with an idea that, in the current voting design, users can "upvote" or "downvote" a path showing their likes or dislikes, but those “dislikes” may hurt the author who spent a long time writing the path. So I remove the “downvote” button.
