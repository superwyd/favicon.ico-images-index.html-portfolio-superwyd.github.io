Usquirrel


SPA Web Development

Dec 2016 - Present

A company official Single Page Application website design and development for Usquirrel, which is an educational consulting company providing Chinese high school students ways studying in USA.

Details

The company official website need to be redesigned.

The slogan page under the homepage was not consistent enough, also the graphs didn’t related to the company information closely.





The quick introduction section design was too simple and not attractive enough.





The successful experience section was just in normal format, not creative enough.





Besides, Usquirrel is a company providing students who don’t have strong English background a way to study abroad, helping them applying to community universities first and trying to transfer to top ranking universities. So the main targets are International (Chinese) students who want to study abroad and their parents. In the new design, we add English title for each Chinese main title, trying to add more "international" elements.

We provide several additional new sections as follows:







We use these two “What is Usquirrel” and “Why Choose Us” sections instead of the slogan section to introduce our main business and major advantages in order to encourage clients to collaborate with us rather than with other similar type companies.

In the “Why Choose Us” section, users can figure out Usquirrel’s advantages and services in different aspects with just hovering their mouse on different squares. Instead of listing everything in three separate tabs inline, the new display is more concise and attractive.





The new design of “Successful Experience” section uses a similar way as “Why choose us” above, it displays the same information in more compact and intriguing way. Only when you hover on the five photos separately can you get the detailed information about these cases.

I take charge of the development on “Why choose us” and “Successful Experience” sections. We are developing this website with Vue.js framework. While programming on Successful Experience section, I realized that when users are hovering their mouse, only the above content is changed, however, the bottom part displaying five photos is not clear enough. In order to let users have a more legible idea on which photo they were hovering on, I suggested to add a "zoom" feature while hovering.
