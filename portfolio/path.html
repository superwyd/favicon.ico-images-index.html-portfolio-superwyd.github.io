Path


Web Development/Design

Feb 2017-present

A place for everyone to ask and answer all kinds of learning path. Users are also very welcome to share their own learning path here.

Inspiration

Nowadays we have many kinds of websites providing a platform for users to ask questions and share experiences such as Quora and Zhihu. But many person around me always complain that if they want to search a specific learning stuff on these sites, the results are sometimes confusing, they still have no idea where to start learning. So Shihao Xia (charlesxsh.github.io) and I want to make a website for users to share learning paths step by step. In our website, users can search for the learning path they want and check out the results in a much clearer way.

HomePage

While sketching out the wireframe of the home page, I used different color representing different areas and provided three designs as follows:



I did a preference test on 42 testers from different countries, ages, and jobs and let them choose their favorite and most dislike home page design and also give me choosing reason and feedback. The result turned out to be 15 testers preferred the first design, 4 testers preferred the second design, and 13 testers preferred the last one. The number of testers who preferred the first one and last one are too close.

Also in the feedback comments they provided, I realized that those testers who didn’t like the first design mostly because they thought the user-info section floating on the right was discordant; those testers who didn’t like the second design mostly because there were too much information on the homepage and the main part was not straightforward enough; those testers who didn’t like the third design mostly because, although the interface looked clean, the position of searching bar was a little bit discordant. And another two common feedbacks were moving the postion of “DownloadApp”, “About US” and “Contact Us” to the footer of the website avoiding distraction and the position of top three buttons “Ask” “Answer” “Share Path” were not comfortable.

Based on all the information I gained, I modified my home page with the fourth design and then created the prototype base on it and built its final UI as follows:



Trend

I designed two types of Trend page and tested on the same group of testers:



As a result, 35 testers preferred the first design. Their comments on the second one was too much spaces wasted. So I designed the Trend page based on the left design and created the final UI. Trend page has three parts: Question, Path and Topic, which represent the most popular questions, paths, and topics seperately.
